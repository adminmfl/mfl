Activities & Challenges

Two simple tools.
One lively community.

Every MFL league runs on just two ingredients — Activities that everyone does daily and Challenges that fire up the competition. Your RWA host picks the mix at setup; MFL handles everything else.

Activities are the backbone of the league — running every day from Day 1 to Day 40. Each resident logs their own activity and earns points for their wing or team. Age-appropriate targets mean everyone can contribute meaningfully.

Questions

What societies
always ask us.

What's the difference between Activities and Challenges?
Activities run every day — individual habits like morning walks or yoga that each resident logs to earn points for their wing. Challenges are short team events (2–5 days) that the host deploys for excitement — a Table Tennis Tournament, Plank Relay, or Steps Matchup between wings. Activities build the habit; Challenges build the drama.
How do we choose activities for our society?
At setup the RWA host picks 1–3 daily activities — steps, yoga, walking, cycling, swimming, and more. Each activity has age-adjusted targets so an 8-year-old and a 70-year-old both earn meaningful points. The AI League Manager recommends the right mix based on your community profile.
Can children participate?
Yes — children from age 8 upwards participate under a parent's account. Activities have age-appropriate targets so a child's 3,000 steps earns the same points as an adult's 8,000. Everyone contributes to the team meaningfully regardless of age.
What about elderly residents who aren't tech-savvy?
Family members can log activities on behalf of elderly participants. The app is intentionally simple — one tap to log. Many seniors in our pilots became the most active participants once their grandchildren showed them the leaderboard.
How are teams formed — by wing, tower, or random?
You choose at setup. Wing vs wing uses the natural geography of your society and works brilliantly for larger communities. Cross-wing random teams mix neighbours and work better for smaller societies where meeting new people is the primary goal.
Does the RWA committee need to manage it daily?
No. The AI League Manager handles daily reminders, points updates, challenge deployments, and leaderboard communications automatically. Under 30 minutes a week from the committee is typical — mostly enjoying watching the society come alive.
Do residents need to download an app?
No App Store needed. MFL installs as a Progressive Web App from the browser in one tap. Works on all Android and iOS devices regardless of phone model or storage space.
How does the free trial work?
Your league goes live and runs free for the first 7 days — teams are active, the leaderboard is live, residents are logging. No card required upfront. If the society loves it (they will), you pay to unlock the rest of the season. If not, you walk away owing nothing.
